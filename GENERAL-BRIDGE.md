# What You Should Already Know

A quick recap of General-class concepts that the Extra exam builds on. If any of these feel shaky, review them before diving into the Extra study guides.

---

## HF Operating: Bands, Privileges, and Modes

From General, you know:

- HF bands run from **160 meters (1.8 MHz) through 10 meters (28 MHz)**
- Each band has sub-bands for CW, digital, and phone — and General-class operators have specific frequency privileges within each
- **USB** is used on 20 meters and above; **LSB** on 40 meters and below
- FT8, PSK31, RTTY, and Winlink are common digital modes on HF

**What Extra adds:** You gain full access to every amateur HF band — no more restricted sub-bands. The Extra exam tests your understanding of exclusive Extra-class segments, the specific frequency limits for each mode on each band, and ITU Region differences. Covered in **E1**.

---

## Impedance, Reactance, and Resonance

From General (G5), you know:

- **Reactance** is opposition to AC from capacitors (Xc) and inductors (XL)
- **Impedance (Z)** combines resistance and reactance: Z = sqrt(R² + X²)
- At **resonance**, XL = Xc, and impedance is purely resistive
- A **low SWR** means the antenna impedance matches the feed line (typically 50 ohms)

**What Extra adds:** Complex impedance using **rectangular (R + jX)** and **polar** notation. Smith Charts for visualizing impedance and designing matching networks. Q factor, bandwidth calculations, phase angles, and skin effect. This is the math-heaviest section of the Extra exam. Covered in **E5**.

## Circuit Components and Design

From General (G6), you know:

- Capacitors store energy in electric fields; inductors store it in magnetic fields
- Diodes conduct in one direction; LEDs emit light
- Transistors amplify signals (BJT and FET basics)
- Regulators maintain stable voltage

**What Extra adds:** Op-amp circuits (gain, feedback, virtual ground), filter design (Butterworth, Chebyshev, elliptical), oscillator types (Colpitts, Hartley, crystal), mixer theory, and MMICs. You'll need to analyze multi-stage circuits and understand how components interact in transmitter and receiver chains. Covered in **E6** and **E7**.

---

## Antennas

From General (G9), you know:

- A half-wave dipole is the fundamental reference antenna
- Antenna gain is measured in **dBi** (over isotropic) or **dBd** (over dipole)
- Feed point impedance matters for matching
- Yagi-Uda antennas use directors and reflectors for gain and directivity

**What Extra adds:** Antenna modeling and analysis — radiation patterns, beamwidth, front-to-back ratio, polarization, and effective radiated power calculations. Log-periodic arrays, phased verticals, stacking, rhombic antennas, and detailed Yagi design. You'll work with antenna patterns in both azimuth and elevation. Covered in **E9**.

## Propagation

From General (G3), you know:

- HF signals refract off the **ionosphere** (D, E, F1, F2 layers)
- **MUF** (Maximum Usable Frequency) determines the highest frequency that will return to Earth
- Solar activity (sunspot cycle, solar flux) directly affects HF propagation
- The **K-index** and **A-index** measure geomagnetic disturbance

**What Extra adds:** Transequatorial propagation, long-path vs. short-path, gray-line propagation, scatter modes (tropospheric, meteor, EME/moonbounce), and ducting. You'll need to understand propagation prediction tools and how specific ionospheric conditions affect different bands. Covered in **E3**.

## Safety

From General (G0), you know:

- **RF exposure** must stay within FCC MPE limits
- MPE limits depend on frequency, power, distance, and duty cycle
- Antenna installations must comply with local codes
- Tower climbing requires proper safety equipment

**What Extra adds:** Specific RF exposure calculations for various antenna types and environments, near-field vs. far-field exposure, controlled vs. uncontrolled environments, and averaging time. You'll also encounter questions about high-voltage safety in amplifier circuits and proper grounding for lightning protection. Covered in **E0**.

---

## Regulations

From General (G1), you know:

- Band plans define frequency allocations and mode restrictions
- **Third-party traffic** rules vary by country
- Volunteer Examiners administer license exams
- The FCC enforces Part 97 rules

**What Extra adds:** Deeper regulatory knowledge — satellite frequency coordination, spread spectrum rules, space station operation, remote control of stations, automatic message forwarding, and the specific privileges that come with an Extra-class license. You'll also encounter questions about ITU Radio Regulations and international operating agreements. Covered in **E1**.

## Quick Self-Test

Can you answer these without looking them up?

1. What happens to impedance at resonance in a series RLC circuit? *(It equals the resistance — reactances cancel)*
2. What does a Smith Chart display? *(Complex impedance and reflection coefficient)*
3. Which sideband is conventionally used on 20 meters? *(USB)*
4. What is the K-index? *(A measure of geomagnetic disturbance, 0-9 scale)*
5. What type of antenna uses both directors and a reflector? *(Yagi-Uda)*

If you got 4-5 right, you're ready to start the Extra study guides. If you struggled, spend some time reviewing the General material — the Extra exam builds directly on these foundations.
